# **1\. Common Questions**

These are the best questions to use in the **first-round intake**, since nearly all processors will require them in some form.

## **A. Legal Business Information**

1. What is your legal business name?  
2. What is your DBA / operating / trade name?  
3. What type of business entity are you?  
4. What is your business registration / corporation / GST/HST number?  
5. When was the business established or incorporated?  
6. What is your legal business address?  
7. Is your operating address different from your legal address?  
8. What is your business phone number?  
9. What is your legal business email?  
10. What is your website URL?  
11. What products or services do you sell?  
12. Please describe your business in detail.

## **B. Business Model**

13. What type of merchant are you?  
14. Do you sell physical goods, digital goods, services, or a mix?  
15. Are your customers B2B, B2C, or both?  
16. Is payment taken in advance before fulfillment?  
17. Do you offer recurring billing or subscriptions?  
18. How long does it usually take for customers to receive the product or service?

## **C. Ownership / Control**

19. Please list all beneficial owners with 25% or more ownership.  
20. Is the business owned by another company or parent entity?  
21. Is there anyone with significant managerial control who is not an owner?  
22. Who is the authorized signer for this application?  
23. Is the signer one of the owners listed above?

## **D. Processing History**

24. Do you currently process card payments?  
25. Who is your current or previous processor?  
26. Why are you leaving your current / previous processor?  
27. Has the business or any owner ever had a merchant account or processing agreement terminated?  
28. Has the business or any owner ever filed for bankruptcy?  
29. Has the business or any owner ever been identified in a Visa / Mastercard risk program?

## **E. Sales Profile**

30. What is your estimated monthly processing volume?  
31. What is your average transaction amount?  
32. What is your highest transaction amount?  
33. What percentage of your transactions are card present, e-commerce, and MOTO / keyed?  
34. Which payment types do you want to accept?  
35. What percentage of your transactions are recurring?  
36. What percentage of your transactions involve foreign cards?

## **F. Website / Compliance**

37. Does your website include a Privacy Policy?  
38. Does your website include Terms and Conditions / Terms of Use?  
39. Does your website include a Return / Refund Policy?  
40. Does your website include customer service contact information?  
41. Is your payment page encrypted with SSL or better?

## **G. PCI / Risk / Security**

42. Do you store credit card numbers?  
43. Do you use any third-party applications to process, transmit, or store cardholder data?  
44. Have you experienced a data breach or card data compromise in the past?  
45. Is your business an MSB or another regulated business?

## **H. Core Documents**

46. Can you provide Business Registration or Articles of Incorporation?  
47. Can you provide a Void Cheque or Bank Letter?  
48. Can you provide 2 recent official business bank statements?  
49. Can you provide proof of business address?  
50. Can you provide proof of ownership?  
51. Can each 25%+ owner and the signer provide government-issued photo ID?  
52. If you currently process payments, can you provide 3 recent processing statements?

# **4\. Chase-Specific Questions**

This group is most distinctive for **ownership-structure branching, 10%+ owners, senior manager logic, guarantors, and advance-payment granularity**.

## **A. Ownership Structure Logic**

1. Is the business privately owned, publicly traded, government, non-profit, sole proprietorship, or owned by a parent entity?  
2. Who are the two owners with the greatest ownership percentages?  
3. Are there any additional owners with 10% or greater ownership, directly or indirectly?  
4. Is there anyone not listed who can make financial decisions or control company policy?

## **B. Owner / Delegate / Senior Manager**

5. Is there an authorized delegate or representative?  
6. Who is the senior manager for the business?  
7. What title does the senior manager hold?  
8. If the senior manager is not already listed, can you complete an addendum?

## **C. Payment Timing / Recurring Granularity**

9. Is payment taken in advance of when goods or services are received?  
10. If yes, what percentage of total processing sales is paid in advance?  
11. Of those advance payments, what percentage falls into:  
* 1–7 days  
* 8–14 days  
* 15–30 days  
* Over 30 days  
12. Is billing recurring?  
13. If yes, what percentage of sales is recurring?  
14. What is the recurring billing breakdown?  
* 30 days  
* 60 days  
* 90 days  
* Annually  
* Other

## **D. Sales Breakdown**

15. What percentage of annual payment card transactions are:  
* card present  
* keyed / mail / phone order  
* e-commerce  
16. Which payment methods do you wish to accept?  
17. Do you already have an American Express Merchant Number?

## **E. Signer / Guarantee / Consent**

18. Who is the authorized representative signing on behalf of the merchant?  
19. Is the signer listed in the ownership section?  
20. Is a personal guarantee required?  
21. Who are the guarantors?  
22. Does each guarantor agree to the personal guarantee terms?  
23. Does the merchant consent to credit and financial investigation?  
24. Does the merchant consent to pre-authorized debits from the settlement account?

## **F. Reporting / Retrieval**

25. Where should chargeback and retrieval requests be sent?  
26. Should online reporting instructions be sent to the legal email provided?

# **5\. Smart Intake Optimization**

The master list should remain the **source of truth for coverage**, but the live intake should not ask every question up front.  
The better pattern is:

1. Ask the **5 anchor questions** first.  
2. Use AI to choose the **next section order**.  
3. Inside each section, reveal only the **follow-up questions that are actually relevant**.  
4. Save processor-specific detail for **after AI routing / admin review**.

## **A. Questions That Should Always Stay Early**

These are the minimum fields that are almost always required and should remain near the front:

- legal business name  
- entity type  
- registration / incorporation number  
- established date  
- legal address  
- business phone / legal email  
- product / service description  
- business model  
- customer type  
- advance payment yes/no  
- recurring yes/no  
- beneficial owners 25%+  
- authorized signer  
- current processing yes/no  
- adverse history yes/no  
- average ticket / highest ticket  
- transaction channel split  
- foreign-card exposure  

## **B. Questions That Should Be Conditional**

These should only appear when a previous answer makes them relevant:

- operating address: only if operating address is different  
- parent company name: only if parent-owned  
- non-owner controller details: only if one exists  
- current processor and exit reason: only if currently processing cards  
- termination / bankruptcy / risk-program explanation: only if the answer is yes  
- recurring percentage: only if recurring billing is yes  
- website policy / SSL questions: only if the merchant has a website, e-commerce, MOTO, digital delivery, or recurring billing  
- processing statements readiness: only if the merchant already processes payments

## **C. How To Keep It Short Without Losing Coverage**

The intake should use **progressive disclosure**:

- ask the category question first  
- ask the detail only when the category answer creates a risk, compliance, or underwriting need  
- avoid asking processor-specific questions in the common layer  
- avoid duplicate collection of the same fact in different wording  
- capture “yes/no + explanation if yes” instead of forcing every merchant into a long free-text form

## **D. Recommended Live Intake Shape**

**Phase 1 — 5 anchors**

- business type  
- country  
- industry  
- monthly volume  
- monthly transaction count  

**Phase 2 — adaptive common intake**

- Legal business information  
- Business model  
- Ownership and control  
- Processing history  
- Sales profile  
- Website / compliance only when relevant  
- Document readiness only for documents that apply

**Phase 3 — KYC / KYB routing**

- decide who needs verification and which entity docs are required

**Phase 4 — document upload**

- ask only for required or missing uploads

**Phase 5 — processor-specific follow-up**

- Nuvei / Payroc / Chase detail only after routing or admin review

## **E. Design Principle**

The system should feel like:

- **short for simple merchants**
- **deeper only when risk, complexity, or processor policy requires it**
- **complete enough for underwriting without forcing every merchant through the longest path**
